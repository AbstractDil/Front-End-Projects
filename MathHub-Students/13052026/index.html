Equilateral Triangle
1.
Height: $h = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} a$
2.
Area: $A = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4} a^2$
3.
Perimeter: $P = 3a$
Right Angle Triangle
1.
Area: $A = \frac{1}{2} \times b \times h$
2.
Perimeter: $P = a + b + c$
3.
Hypotenuse: $c = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}$
Isosceles Triangle
1.
Height: $h = \sqrt{a^2 - \frac{b^2}{4}}$
2.
Area: $A = \frac{b}{4} \sqrt{4a^2 - b^2}$
3.
Perimeter: $P = 2a + b$
General Triangle (Heron's Formula)

Used when all three sides ($a, b, c$) are known.

1.
Semi-perimeter: $s = \frac{a+b+c}{2}$
2.
Area: $A = \sqrt{s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)}$
